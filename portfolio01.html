Marketing & Advertising Officer ｜ 2016-2017

Re-Launch Campaign

PROBLEM

A society with a reputation for being too 'serious' was not getting new signups, and had trouble standing out from the other socieities on campus.

SOLUTION

Open the semester with creative topics, communicating in the spaces the students are, and showcase the society's value proposition in a less serious environment.

COMEDY DEBATES

Hosting a comedy debates and casual conversation outlets allowed students to get involved with the society and meet its members without being weighed down by a serious topic.

FACEBOOK ADS

The social network that was most popular with students for organising events, this was the primary channel of communication.

Taking advantage of Facebook ads to target students on campus, it was one of the first socities on campus to use paid ads on social media.

ACCESSIBILITY

The method of signups was taken from a pen and paper method to a online signup form, allowing new members to sign up instantly instead of waiting for signup desks to pop up twice a semester.

This also allowed for a mailing list for all members, opening a second channel of communication directly with members, and established a database that could inform future committees.

LIGHT-ARTED

The artwork to support the activities had a casual, playful tone, designed to stand out from the other socities but also with a level of quality that would indicate an established group.
